About Me

I am Kayden Chow, a dedicated student at Tampines Secondary School in Singapore, currently in Class 4E3. I am passionate about contributing to my school and community through various initiatives, leadership roles, and co-curricular activities. With over 70 hours of Values in Action (VIA) projects, I have developed a strong sense of responsibility, teamwork, and empathy. My experiences have shaped me into a more confident and outgoing individual, ready to take on new challenges.

Values in Action (VIA) Projects

I have accumulated over 70 hours of VIA projects through my co-curricular activities and class initiatives. One of my most rewarding experiences was visiting the NTUC Nursing Home, where I spent time with the elderly, engaging in conversations and activities to brighten their day. These interactions taught me the importance of empathy and active listening, leaving a lasting impact on my perspective toward community service.

Additionally, I represented Tampines Secondary School twice in "Our Schools, Our Stories," a local event involving multiple schools. This platform allowed me to showcase my school's values and connect with students from diverse backgrounds, further enriching my understanding of collaboration and community engagement.

Co-Curricular Activities (CCA)

As part of my CCA, I play a key role in backstage coordination for school events, including concerts and cultural celebrations such as Chinese New Year, Hari Raya, and Deepavali. My responsibilities include rehearsing after school and ensuring smooth execution of all movements during these events. This role has honed my organizational skills and ability to work under pressure.

I also participated in Outward Bound Singapore, an event that broadened my perspective through interactions with students from other schools. This experience enhanced my teamwork and social skills, teaching me how to collaborate effectively in diverse groups.

Furthermore, I competed in two inter-class football games, which were televised on the school's YouTube channel. Although my team did not win, the experience strengthened my resilience and teamwork, as we worked together to strive for success.

Leadership Roles

For two years, I served as a Peer Support Leader in Class 4E3, working alongside two other leaders to conduct weekly briefings and present our class's peer support project to the entire school. This role required me to engage with my peers on topics such as mental health, offering support and encouragement. Presenting on stage and leading discussions helped me become more open, outgoing, and confident in public speaking.

Applied Design Thinking Course Write-Up

During the holidays, I participated in a 2-day Applied Design Thinking course at Temasek Polytechnic, an enriching experience that allowed me to collaborate with both familiar friends and students from other classes I had not previously interacted with. The course provided a platform to share my thoughts openly, give and receive constructive feedback, and engage in creative problem-solving. Through group activities, I honed my communication skills, learning how to efficiently coordinate with teammates to complete tasks. The culminating project required us to present our findings and design solutions, which strengthened my ability to articulate ideas clearly and work effectively in a team. This course not only enhanced my collaborative and creative skills but also boosted my confidence in contributing to group discussions and projects.
